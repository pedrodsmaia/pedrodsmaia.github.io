Research

Research on technology, governance, and political life.

Pedro Maia is a Political Science, International Relations, and Science and Technology Studies scholar whose work examines the political, ethical, and social dimensions of technological innovation and governance.

  • PhD in International Relations and Political Science, Geneva Graduate Institute, 2024
  • Postdoctoral Researcher at SKEMA Business School, funded by Agence Nationale de la Recherche
  • Lecturer in Technology and International Relations at the European School of Political and Social Sciences

Approach

Research that moves across academia, policy, and public debate.

His research focuses on how technology companies, governments, and international organizations interact in the governance of emerging technologies, with attention to interfaces, AI diplomacy, digital infrastructures, and regulatory politics.

  • Thesis: Prefiguration and GIS Interfaces: affects, politics and decision-making
  • Current themes include AI governance, science diplomacy, digital security, and the politics of interfaces
  • Previous work also explored violence, quantification, and security in Latin America
